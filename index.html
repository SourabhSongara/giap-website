About the CIA® Certification

The Certified Internal Auditor (CIA) is the only globally recognized certification for internal auditors. It has a 50-year history and is the standard for proving your professional competency in internal auditing.

Being a CIA means you can confidently perform any audit, for anyone, anywhere around the world.

Key Benefits:

  • Enhance credibility and respect.
  • Increase advancement and earning potential (CIA holders can earn 51% more*).
  • Sharpen professional skills and proficiencies.
  • Demonstrate understanding and commitment to the profession.

*According to The IIA's 2017 Internal Audit Compensation Study (based on U.S. responses).

Eligibility Requirements

Candidates must meet specific educational and work experience criteria to apply for the CIA certification.

Educational Requirements Work Experience (or Equivalent)
Bachelor's Degree (or equivalent) 2 Years of internal audit experience
Master's Degree (or equivalent) 1 Year of internal audit experience
Active Internal Audit Practitioner Designation Holder 5 Years of internal audit experience
5 Years of Internal Audit Experience (or equivalent) No Additional Experience Requirement

Special Notes:

Students can take CIA exams while pursuing college studies. CAs and CA-first attempt candidates seeking global career opportunities can also apply.

Candidates must complete the papers and the certification process within 3 years.
